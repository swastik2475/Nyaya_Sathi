Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023 — Overview
The Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023 replaced the Indian Penal Code (IPC) 1860 with effect from July 1, 2024. It modernises criminal law in India and introduces several new provisions.

BNS Section 103 — Murder
Punishment for murder is death or life imprisonment with fine. The BNS retains the death penalty for murder.

BNS Section 111 — Organised Crime
Any person who is a member of, or organises or directs activities of, an organised crime syndicate commits an offence under BNS. Punishment includes life imprisonment or death if the offence results in death.

BNS Section 113 — Terrorism
BNS for the first time includes terrorism as an offence punishable with death or life imprisonment, bringing it into the mainstream criminal code.

BNS Section 63 — Rape
BNS Section 63 replaces IPC 376. Whoever commits rape shall be punished with rigorous imprisonment for a term not less than ten years, which may extend to imprisonment for life. Minimum 20 years for gang rape.

BNS Section 84 — Sedition (Replaced)
The old sedition provision (IPC 124A) has been scrapped. BNS Section 152 penalises acts endangering sovereignty, unity and integrity of India — a narrower and more precise definition.

BNS Section 79 — Outraging Modesty
Replaces IPC 354. Punishes assault or criminal force against women with intent to outrage modesty.

BNS Section 86 — Theft
Replaces IPC 379. Punishment for theft is imprisonment up to 3 years or fine or both.

BNS Section 303 — Property Offences (Theft Definition)
Theft under BNS means dishonestly taking any moveable property out of the possession of any person without that person's consent.

Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS) 2023 — Criminal Procedure
The BNSS 2023 replaced the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC) 1973 from July 1, 2024. Key changes include:
- Trials must conclude within 3 years.
- Charge sheet must be filed within 90 days of arrest (extendable to 180 days).
- Electronic FIR and summons now recognised.
- Provision for Zero FIR — an FIR can be filed at any police station regardless of jurisdiction.
- Audio-video recording of search and seizure operations is now mandatory.
- Handcuffing is permitted only in specific circumstances (notorious offenders, repeat offenders, those likely to escape).

BNSS Section 173 — FIR (First Information Report)
Every information relating to a cognisable offence must be reduced to writing by the officer in charge of a police station and be read over to the informant. A copy must be given to the informant free of charge. If a police officer refuses to register an FIR, the person may send the substance of the information in writing and by post to the Superintendent of Police. Online (e-FIR) registration is also available at the police portal.

BNSS Section 479 — Bail for Undertrial Prisoners
An undertrial prisoner who has completed half the maximum period of imprisonment for the offence they are charged with (one-third if first-time offender) is entitled to bail. The Magistrate must be satisfied that the person is not a flight risk or danger to society.

Consumer Rights in India — Consumer Protection Act 2019

Six Consumer Rights Under Indian Law
1. Right to Safety: Protection against goods and services hazardous to life and property.
2. Right to Information: Right to be informed about quality, quantity, potency, purity, standard and price of goods or services.
3. Right to Choose: Access to a variety of goods and services at competitive prices.
4. Right to be Heard: Assurance that consumer interests will receive due consideration at appropriate forums.
5. Right to Seek Redressal: Right to redressal against unfair trade practices or restrictive trade practices or unscrupulous exploitation of consumers.
6. Right to Consumer Education: Right to acquire knowledge and skills to be an informed consumer.

How to File a Consumer Complaint in India
Consumer complaints are handled by Consumer Disputes Redressal Commissions (Consumer Courts) at three levels:
- District Commission: Claims up to ₹50 lakh.
- State Commission: Claims between ₹50 lakh and ₹2 crore.
- National Commission (NCDRC): Claims above ₹2 crore.

Steps to File a Consumer Complaint:
Step 1: Send a legal notice to the seller/service provider first (optional but recommended).
Step 2: If unresolved, file a complaint at the appropriate Consumer Commission.
Step 3: Attach supporting documents (receipts, warranty cards, correspondence, photos).
Step 4: Pay the prescribed fee (nominal, based on claim amount).
Step 5: The commission will send a notice to the opposite party and schedule a hearing.

Online Consumer Complaint Portal: https://consumerhelpline.gov.in (National Consumer Helpline: 1800-11-4000 or 14404 — toll free)

E-Daakhil Portal: https://edaakhil.nic.in — file consumer complaints online from anywhere in India.

Limitation Period: A consumer complaint must be filed within 2 years from the date on which the cause of action arises.

Unfair Trade Practices
Under the Consumer Protection Act 2019, the following are unfair trade practices:
- False representation of quality, grade, composition, or standard of goods.
- False or misleading advertisements.
- Refusal to issue a bill or receipt.
- Warranties not honoured.
- Selling at prices higher than the Maximum Retail Price (MRP).

Product Liability (New under 2019 Act)
Manufacturers, service providers, and sellers are now liable for harm caused by defective products even without proof of negligence (strict liability). Compensation can be claimed for personal injury, death, or property damage.

E-Commerce Consumer Rights
E-commerce companies must:
- Display contact information, return/refund policy, and grievance redressal mechanism on their website.
- Resolve complaints within 48 hours of acknowledgement and within 1 month of receipt.
- Not engage in unfair trade practices.

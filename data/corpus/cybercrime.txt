Cybercrime in India — Laws and Reporting

What is Cybercrime?
Cybercrime refers to any criminal activity involving computers, networks, or the internet. It includes hacking, online fraud, identity theft, cyberbullying, child pornography, phishing, ransomware, and online harassment.

Laws Governing Cybercrime in India
1. Information Technology Act 2000 (IT Act) — primary law for cybercrimes.
2. Indian Penal Code (IPC) / Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) — applicable for cybercrimes involving fraud, cheating, defamation.
3. POCSO Act 2012 — child sexual abuse material online.

Key IT Act Sections
- Section 43: Penalty for unauthorised access, damage to computer systems — compensation up to ₹1 crore.
- Section 65: Tampering with computer source code — imprisonment up to 3 years or fine ₹2 lakh or both.
- Section 66: Computer-related offences (hacking) — imprisonment up to 3 years or fine ₹5 lakh or both.
- Section 66A: Sending offensive messages (struck down by Supreme Court in Shreya Singhal v. Union of India, 2015 — now unconstitutional).
- Section 66B: Dishonestly receiving stolen computer resources — imprisonment up to 3 years or fine ₹1 lakh.
- Section 66C: Identity theft — imprisonment up to 3 years + fine ₹1 lakh.
- Section 66D: Cheating by impersonation using computer — imprisonment up to 3 years + fine ₹1 lakh.
- Section 66E: Violation of privacy (publishing private images without consent) — imprisonment up to 3 years + fine ₹2 lakh.
- Section 66F: Cyber terrorism — life imprisonment.
- Section 67: Publishing obscene material online — imprisonment up to 3 years.
- Section 67A: Publishing sexually explicit material — imprisonment up to 5 years + fine.
- Section 67B: Publishing child pornography — imprisonment up to 7 years + fine.

How to Report Cybercrime in India
1. National Cybercrime Reporting Portal: https://www.cybercrime.gov.in
   - Report financial fraud, online fraud, social media crimes, cyber harassment, child pornography.
   - Available 24/7.
2. Cybercrime Helpline: 1930 (call to report online financial fraud — immediate action possible).
3. Local Cyber Cell: File FIR at your nearest police station or dedicated cyber police station.

Step-by-step Online Reporting:
Step 1: Visit https://www.cybercrime.gov.in
Step 2: Click "Report Cybercrime" and select the category.
Step 3: Fill in your personal details and incident details.
Step 4: Upload evidence (screenshots, transaction receipts, email headers).
Step 5: Submit — you will receive an acknowledgement number.

What to Do Immediately After Online Financial Fraud
1. Call 1930 within the first few hours — the helpline can freeze/block transactions.
2. Inform your bank immediately to block the account/card.
3. File a complaint on the cybercrime portal.
4. Save all evidence (screenshots, call recordings, transaction details).

Types of Common Cybercrimes and What to Do
- OTP Fraud / Vishing: Never share OTP. If shared, call 1930 immediately and block account.
- Phishing: Do not click suspicious links. Forward phishing emails to cybercrime cell.
- Sextortion / Morphed Images: Report under Section 66E IT Act and POCSO (if minor). Contact police or Cybercrime Cell.
- Online Shopping Fraud: Report on cybercrime portal + consumer helpline 1800-11-4000.
- Social Media Hacking: Report to the platform + cybercrime portal.
- Cyberbullying: Screenshot evidence. Report to cybercrime portal + school/college authorities.

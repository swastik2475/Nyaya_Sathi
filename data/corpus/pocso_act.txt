POCSO Act — Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act 2012

What is POCSO?
The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act 2012 is a comprehensive law to protect children below the age of 18 years from sexual abuse, sexual harassment, and pornography. It was amended in 2019 to increase punishments.

Key Definitions
- Child: Any person below the age of 18 years.
- Penetrative Sexual Assault (Section 3-4): Any penetration of sexual organ by any body part or object into the vagina, mouth, urethra, or anus of a child. Punishment: minimum 10 years rigorous imprisonment, may extend to life imprisonment + fine.
- Aggravated Penetrative Sexual Assault (Section 5-6): When committed by a person in a position of trust or authority (teacher, police, relative, doctor). Punishment: minimum 20 years to life imprisonment, or death penalty.
- Sexual Assault (Section 7-8): Touching sexual organs with sexual intent without penetration. Minimum 3 years imprisonment.
- Sexual Harassment (Section 11-12): Showing pornography, indecent/obscene speech, etc. Minimum 3 years imprisonment.
- Child Pornography (Section 14-15): Using child for pornographic purposes is punishable with minimum 5-7 years imprisonment.

Presumption of Guilt (Section 29)
The POCSO Act reverses the normal presumption: once penetrative sexual assault is alleged, the accused is presumed guilty unless proven innocent. This protects child victims.

Special Courts
POCSO requires designation of Special Courts to try cases. The trial must be conducted in-camera (closed court), child-friendly, and should be completed within 1 year.

Mandatory Reporting (Section 19-21)
Any person (not just law enforcement) who has apprehension that a sexual offence is likely to be committed, or has knowledge that such an offence has been committed, MUST report it to the Special Juvenile Police Unit (SJPU) or local police. Failure to report is punishable with imprisonment up to 6 months or fine.

Police cannot refuse to register an FIR for a POCSO offence.

Child-Friendly Procedures
- Child's statement must be recorded by a woman police officer or magistrate.
- Medical examination must be conducted in the presence of a parent or guardian.
- Court must provide a child-friendly atmosphere (no wigs, no harsh questioning).
- Identity of the victim must not be disclosed by media or any person.
- Free legal aid is provided to the child victim.

False Complaint
Filing a false complaint under POCSO with malicious intent is punishable with imprisonment and fine.

Cyber POCSO (Amendment 2019)
Child pornography — even storing/watching it (Section 15) — is now a criminal offence punishable with 3-5 years imprisonment. Reporting child pornography online: https://www.cybercrime.gov.in

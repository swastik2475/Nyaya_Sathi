Property Registration in India

Why Register Property?
Property registration under the Registration Act 1908 is mandatory for immovable property transactions above ₹100 in value. Failure to register means the document is not admissible as evidence of title and the transfer is not legally complete.

Documents Required for Property Registration
1. Sale deed / conveyance deed (drafted by a lawyer)
2. Title documents (previous chain of ownership)
3. Encumbrance Certificate (EC) — proves property is free of loans
4. Property card / Khata / 7/12 extract (varies by state)
5. Identity proof of buyer and seller (Aadhaar, PAN)
6. PAN card (mandatory for transactions above ₹10 lakh)
7. Form 26QB (TDS on property if consideration exceeds ₹50 lakh)
8. No-Objection Certificate (NOC) if applicable (CHS society, etc.)
9. Power of Attorney (if any party is acting through representative)

Step-by-Step Property Registration Process
Step 1 — Due Diligence: Verify title by checking Encumbrance Certificate at the Sub-Registrar's office (or online in most states) for the past 30 years.
Step 2 — Draft Sale Deed: Get the sale deed drafted by an advocate. Include property description, consideration amount, possession details, warranties.
Step 3 — Pay Stamp Duty: Pay stamp duty at the state government treasury or online (varies by state — typically 4-7% of property value). In Rajasthan: stamp duty is 6% for men, 5% for women.
Step 4 — Book Appointment: Book a slot at the Sub-Registrar's office online (most states: https://igrs.raj.nic.in for Rajasthan; check your state's IGRS portal).
Step 5 — Appear at Sub-Registrar's Office: Both buyer and seller (or their authorised representatives with POA) must appear with two witnesses. Bring all original documents.
Step 6 — Biometric Verification: Thumb impression and photograph of all parties are taken.
Step 7 — Registration: The Sub-Registrar registers the document and returns the registered deed. You receive a registered copy and the document number.
Step 8 — Mutation (Dakhil Kharij): Apply at the local Municipal Corporation / Panchayat to update revenue records in the buyer's name.

Registration Charges
- Registration fee: Generally 1% of the property value (subject to state rules).
- Stamp duty: 4-7% depending on state and gender of buyer.

Online Registration (Rajasthan — IGRS Portal)
Rajasthan's IGRS portal (https://igrs.raj.nic.in) allows:
- Online slot booking for property registration
- Market value checking
- Encumbrance certificate download
- Stamp duty payment

RERA — Real Estate Regulatory Authority
For under-construction properties, check if the project is registered under RERA of your state. RERA protects buyers from project delays, mis-selling, and unfair practices.
- National RERA portal: https://rera.gov.in
- Rajasthan RERA: https://rera.rajasthan.gov.in

Property Dispute Resolution
- Civil courts have jurisdiction over property disputes.
- A suit for specific performance (forcing sale) has a 3-year limitation period.
- RERA Adjudicating Officer handles complaints against builders.

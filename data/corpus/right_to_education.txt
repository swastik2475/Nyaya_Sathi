Right to Education Act 2009 (RTE Act)

Constitutional Basis
Article 21A of the Constitution (inserted by the 86th Constitutional Amendment 2002) makes education a Fundamental Right: "The State shall provide free and compulsory education to all children of the age of six to fourteen years."

The Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act 2009 gives effect to this constitutional mandate.

Key Provisions of the RTE Act

Age Group Covered: Children aged 6 to 14 years (Classes I to VIII).

Free and Compulsory:
- "Free" means no child shall be liable to pay any fee or charges or expenses which may prevent them from pursuing and completing elementary education.
- "Compulsory" means government has the obligation to ensure enrolment, attendance and completion of elementary education.

25% Reservation in Private Schools:
Every private unaided school must admit at least 25% of its class strength at the entry-level class from children belonging to weaker sections and disadvantaged groups. The government reimburses the school for these seats (per-child expenditure or the school fees, whichever is less).

No Detention Policy (Section 16): No child can be expelled or held back in any class up to Class VIII (though states can conduct examinations from Class V onwards post-2019 amendment).

Prohibition of Corporal Punishment (Section 17): No child shall be subjected to physical punishment or mental harassment. The teacher can face disciplinary action.

Age-Appropriate Admission (Section 4): A child above 6 who has not been admitted can be admitted in an age-appropriate class with special training.

Teacher Qualifications and Norms (Section 23): Only qualified teachers as per NCTE (National Council for Teacher Education) norms can be appointed. Pupil-Teacher Ratio must be maintained.

No Screening or Donation (Section 13): Schools cannot charge capitation fee or conduct interviews for admission of child or parents. Violation is punishable with fine.

Grievance Redressal
- Complaints can be filed before the State Commission for Protection of Child Rights (SCPCR).
- National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR) monitors implementation.
- NCPCR helpline: 1800-121-2830 (toll free).

Limitations of RTE Act
- Does not cover children below 6 years (pre-school / Anganwadi under separate schemes like ECCE).
- Does not cover children above 14 years (secondary education separately under Samagra Shiksha).
- Private unaided minority educational institutions are exempt from the 25% reservation requirement (Pramati Educational & Cultural Trust v. Union of India, 2014).

Rights of Women Under Indian Law

Constitutional Rights
- Article 14: Equality before law.
- Article 15: Prohibition of discrimination on grounds of sex.
- Article 15(3): State may make special provisions for women.
- Article 16: Equal opportunity in public employment.
- Article 21: Right to life and dignity includes protection from gender-based violence.
- Article 39(a): State shall ensure equal right to livelihood for men and women.
- Article 42: State shall make provision for maternity relief.

Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act 2005 (PWDVA)
This Act provides civil remedies for women facing domestic violence — physical, sexual, emotional, verbal, and economic abuse. It covers wives, daughters, mothers, live-in partners. Protection orders, residence orders, and monetary relief orders can be sought.

How to File a Complaint for Domestic Violence:
Step 1: Contact a Protection Officer (PO) in your district, or a registered NGO, or go directly to the Magistrate's court.
Step 2: File a Domestic Incident Report (DIR).
Step 3: The Magistrate can issue:
   - Protection Order: prohibiting the abuser from committing acts of violence.
   - Residence Order: ensuring you can live in the shared household.
   - Monetary Relief Order: for medical expenses, loss of earnings.
   - Custody Order: for temporary custody of children.
Step 4: In emergencies, the Magistrate can pass an ex-parte order within 3 days.
Emergency contact: 181 (Women Helpline) or 112 (Police).

Dowry Prohibition Act 1961
Giving or taking dowry is a punishable offence — imprisonment up to 5 years + fine of ₹15,000 or the value of dowry, whichever is higher. IPC Section 498A punishes cruelty by husband/relatives in connection with dowry demands.

Sexual Harassment at Workplace (POSH Act 2013)
The Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal of Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act 2013 applies to all workplaces (public/private). Every employer must:
- Constitute an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC).
- Display the anti-sexual-harassment policy.
- Conduct awareness programmes.
Women can file a complaint with the ICC within 3 months of the incident. Domestic workers can approach the Local Complaints Committee (LCC) of the district.

Maternity Benefit Act 1961 (amended 2017)
- Women working in establishments with 10+ employees are entitled to maternity leave.
- Maternity leave: 26 weeks for first two children, 12 weeks for third child onwards.
- Adoptive/commissioning mothers: 12 weeks.
- Crèche facility mandatory for establishments with 50+ employees.

Vishaka Guidelines (now replaced by POSH Act)
The Supreme Court in Vishaka v. State of Rajasthan (1997) laid down guidelines against sexual harassment at the workplace, which formed the basis for the POSH Act 2013.

Inheritance Rights of Women (Hindu Succession Act 1956, amended 2005)
The 2005 amendment gives daughters the same rights as sons in ancestral/joint family property (coparcenary). A Hindu daughter is now a coparcener by birth with equal right to claim partition.

Muslim Women's Rights
- Triple Talaq (instant) is now a criminal offence (Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Marriage) Act 2019).
- A Muslim woman is entitled to maintenance during iddat period and beyond if she is unable to maintain herself.
- The Supreme Court in Shah Bano case and subsequent legislation protects divorced Muslim women's right to maintenance.

Women's Legal Aid
Free legal aid is available through NALSA (National Legal Services Authority), SLSA (State Legal Services Authority), and DLSA (District Legal Services Authority) for women who cannot afford legal representation.
Helplines:
- 181 — Women Helpline (all India)
- 1091 — Women in Distress
- 1800-180-1516 — NALSA Legal Aid
- 100/112 — Police
